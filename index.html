Geomatics Link Traverser & Traverse Computation

Traverse

A traverse in geomatics (or surveying) refers to a series of connected survey lines whose lengths and directions are measured. These lines form a path or network of points on the ground. Traverses are used to establish control networks, map areas, and precisely measure locations.

Purpose

The main purpose of a traverse is to establish accurate points on the earth's surface, which can be used as reference points in mapping, construction, and other geospatial applications. Traverses are essential for creating base maps and for setting out land boundaries.

Techniques

Techniques employed in this app include open traverse and closed traverse computations. In a closed traverse, the survey returns to the start point, allowing for misclosure calculations. In an open traverse, the start and end points are different, but distances between points can be calculated for accuracy assessments.
